MAC Address A MAC address is a permanent ID number burned into every network device at the factory. It's like a fingerprint that never changes. Format: 12 characters using 0-9 and A-F, separated by colons (example: 3C:22:FB:A3:B4:C5) Find it: Windows: ipconfig /all, Mac: ifconfig


ğŸ²ğŸ“¶ Mac Randomisation

Modern devices create fake MAC addresses for privacy. Instead of using your real permanent MAC address everywhere, your device generates random fake ones at different locations so you can't be tracked across WiFi networks. Why needed: Without this, stores, malls, and coffee shops could track your movements by seeing the same MAC address connecting to their WiFi.
